The Problem

Student hunger is a
hidden crisis

Across Australia's universities, a quiet crisis is unfolding. 1 in 3 students experience food insecurity at some point during their degree — skipping meals, eating less than they need, or going entire days without food.

It's often invisible. Students don't talk about it. They power through lectures on empty stomachs, hide hunger behind the pressure to appear fine, and sacrifice nutrition to cover rent, transport, or textbooks.

This isn't a personal failing. It's a structural problem. The cost of living in Sydney has surged, casual work is unreliable, student allowances haven't kept pace, and many students — particularly international students — have no safety net at all.

And the consequences go far beyond feeling hungry. Malnutrition during university years affects concentration, mental health, academic outcomes, and long-term physical health. When students can't eat properly, they can't thrive. That's why NourishU exists.

Root Causes

Three forces driving
student hunger

Malnutrition among university students isn't random. It's driven by a set of interconnected pressures that, together, make it very hard for students to consistently eat well.

💸

Cost of Living Pressure

Sydney is one of the most expensive cities in the world. For students juggling rent, transport, study materials, and tuition, food is often the first thing cut from the budget.

  • Average Sydney rent for a student: $300–$500/week
  • Average student income (Youth Allowance): ~$360/fortnight
  • Grocery costs have risen sharply since 2022
  • International students often ineligible for government support
🧠

Stress & Mental Load

Academic pressure, social isolation, and anxiety make it harder for students to prioritise eating — and hunger makes all of those things worse. It's a cycle that's difficult to break alone.

  • High academic workloads leave little time for meal prep
  • Stress suppresses appetite and disrupts eating routines
  • Mental health struggles often co-occur with food insecurity
  • Shame and stigma prevent students from seeking support
📖

Lack of Nutritional Knowledge

Many students arrive at university having never cooked for themselves. Without practical knowledge of budget cooking and nutrition, eating well feels out of reach — even when money is available.

  • Reliance on expensive takeaway or processed food
  • No education on meal planning or batch cooking
  • Low awareness of available campus support services
  • Cultural food barriers for international students
The Impact

What malnutrition
actually does

Poor nutrition during university isn't just uncomfortable — it has real, measurable effects on students' ability to learn, function, and build a future.

🧠

Cognitive Decline

Insufficient nutrition impairs memory, concentration, and processing speed — directly undermining academic performance at the worst possible time.

😟

Mental Health

Food insecurity is strongly linked to anxiety, depression, and feelings of hopelessness. Students who are hungry are significantly more likely to experience psychological distress.

📉

Academic Outcomes

Students who experience food insecurity are more likely to fail units, take longer to graduate, or drop out entirely — compounding the very financial stress that caused the problem.

🤒

Physical Health

Skipping meals and poor diet weaken the immune system, reduce energy, and increase risk of long-term conditions — effects that don't disappear after graduation.

🏫

Social Withdrawal

Hunger and shame around food insecurity cause many students to isolate — missing out on study groups, social connections, and the full university experience.

🔮

Future Opportunity

Universities are where futures are built. When students can't engage fully, the ripple effects extend to their careers, earning potential, and lifelong wellbeing.

Our Response

What NourishU does
about it

We take a holistic approach — addressing immediate hunger, the knowledge gap, and the structural barriers that keep students from eating well. Everything we do is free, stigma-free, and built around students' real lives.

01

Free Hot Meal Program Flagship

Our most immediate intervention. We partner with local Sydney restaurants and caterers to deliver freshly prepared, nutritious, culturally diverse hot meals to students on campus multiple times a week. No ID. No means test. No questions asked. You show up — we feed you.

Multiple days/week All Sydney campuses Culturally diverse menus 100% free
02

Campus Food Pantries Access

Stocked walk-in pantry hubs at each of our partner campuses, filled with dry goods, fresh produce, snacks, and hygiene essentials. Open before, between, and after classes — so students can grab what they need without making a special trip or an appointment.

4 Sydney locations Open daily Fresh & dry goods Drop-in, no booking
03

Nutrition Education Workshops Education

Practical, hands-on workshops run by qualified nutritionists and trained peer mentors. Students learn budget meal planning, batch cooking, reading nutrition labels, and how to eat well on a tight income. Tackling the root cause — not just the symptom.

Weekly workshops Budget cooking focus Peer-led sessions Recipe resources
04

Job Connect Program Employment

We work with our partner food businesses to create flexible part-time job opportunities exclusively for NourishU students. This addresses the financial root cause — helping students build income stability alongside their studies, while supporting local Sydney businesses they already know and trust.

80+ business partners Flexible hours Study-friendly rosters 320+ jobs created
05

Emergency Food Packs Crisis Support

For students in immediate crisis, we provide discreet 3-day emergency food packs delivered directly to their campus — no lengthy application process, no judgment. A lifeline for students who need help right now.

Same-week delivery Discreet & private 3-day supply No means test
Our Story

How NourishU started

NourishU began in 2021 when a group of University of Sydney students noticed something they couldn't ignore: their classmates were skipping meals. Not occasionally — regularly. Friends were pretending they'd already eaten. Study groups were losing focus by early afternoon. The signs were everywhere, once you knew to look.

What started as a small weekly meal run from a borrowed campus kitchen grew into something much bigger. Local cafés and restaurants got involved. Volunteers showed up. Word spread. By 2023, we were operating across four Sydney campuses, had launched our pantry network, and had placed over 200 students in part-time jobs with our food business partners.

Today, NourishU is expanding — but our mission hasn't changed. We believe every student deserves to focus on their future without hunger getting in the way. And we believe that communities — students, businesses, universities — are stronger when they look after each other.

Our Values

What we believe

Dignity First

No means tests, no judgment, no bureaucracy. Every student who needs food gets food — with their dignity intact.

Community Over Charity

We don't hand food down — we build communities around it. Students, businesses, and universities are all part of the solution.

Tackling Root Causes

Feeding people matters. So does addressing why they're hungry. We work on both — immediate relief and structural change.

Stigma-Free

Food insecurity is a structural issue, not a personal failure. We work hard to make seeking support feel normal and easy.

Student-Led

Our volunteers, coordinators, and advocates are students themselves. We understand the experience because we live it.

Radical Inclusion

Domestic, international, postgrad, undergrad — if you're a student in need, NourishU is for you. Full stop.
